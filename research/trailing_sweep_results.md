# Trailing-stop head-to-head — does `--trailing-atr` beat the live rule?

_Research only. No live flag, paper-scan, or workflow was touched. Generated by
`research/trailing_sweep.py` (imports the existing `bracket_backtest` + shared
`resolve_bracket`; reimplements nothing)._

## One-line verdict

**NO — do not enable `--trailing-atr` in live.** No trail multiple clears the
project's significance gate (every `boot_p` ≫ 0.05), and the only variant that
"wins" on raw net R/trade (atr=1.0) does so by **gutting 82% of the strategy's
3R runners** — it converts an asymmetric-runner strategy into a tight scalp,
abandoning the actual edge. Keep the trail OFF; it remains a useful offline
re-test, nothing more.

**STEP 0 gate — the ratchet math is real (`resolver.py:137-165`).**
`trail_dist = trailing_atr * atr_at_entry`; each bar `trail_level = extreme −
direction*trail_dist` and `cur_stop = max(cur_stop, trail_level)` (long) /
`min(...)` (short) — the stop **only ratchets toward profit, never loosens**, and
`extreme` updates *after* the stop check (no same-bar look-ahead). Unit-tested in
`tests/test_trailing_sweep.py`.

## Method

- Universe / windows / loader imported verbatim from `scripts/cycle_backtest.py`
  (the validated §41 ~137K-trade study): 10 crypto majors (listing-gated), the
  2018 + 2022 cycle-analog windows + the recent span (2024-06→2026-06),
  `BinanceClient.klines_range` (disk-cached). Restricted to **1h** — the only TF
  the edge survives net-of-fees (§41).
- Signal: the real `confluence_position(min_pct=0.50, trend_align=True)`.
- All figures **net of MAKER fees** (`FEE_PCT = 0.0004` round-trip).
- Population stats come straight from `bracket_backtest` + the engine's own
  `_summarize`. The per-trade beat/match/hurt + runner capture use a **paired**
  pass over the baseline's exact entries, resolved both ways through the shared
  `resolve_bracket` — regression-locked to reproduce `bracket_backtest`'s baseline
  trade list **exactly** (`test_paired_baseline_reproduces_bracket_backtest_exactly`).

## Runner-capture callout (the key risk)

The mechanical "winner," **TRAIL atr=1.0, cuts 393 of 479 former +3R runners short
(82%), for a −479.8R hit on the runner cohort.** Its slim overall positivity
(+0.0064R) comes **entirely from collapsing the left tail** (max DD −216R → −32R),
not from any new edge — it trades the strategy's asymmetric upside for a flat,
tight scalp. The trade-off is monotonic across the sweep: only the **wide** trails
(2.5/3.0) preserve the runners (+388/+452R on that cohort) — but those variants
are net-**negative** over the whole population and equally non-significant. There
is no multiple that both keeps the runners and earns a real edge.

## Honest caveats

1. **No statistical edge anywhere.** Best `boot_p` = 0.293 (atr=1.0) — far from
   the 0.05 gate. Treat "atr=1.0 is positive" as noise, not a result.
2. **Absolute levels did NOT reproduce §41.** My ride-3R reproduction here is
   −0.0151R over n=3540, vs §41's reported +0.096R / n=8,124 on 1h. The trade
   count and sign gap is **unreconciled** (likely evolved features/signal or a
   different cached span) and is flagged, not hidden. The **relative** head-to-head
   (trail vs the two non-trail rules over one identical population) is internally
   valid regardless; the absolute R levels should be read with caution.
3. **Secondary observation (NOT this task's scope, for the owner):** over this
   population the *current live* bank-half/BE/ride-3R rule is the **worst** variant
   (−0.0549R, −216R DD, boot_p 1.000) — materially worse than plain ride-3R. That
   is about the **BE-after-TP1 management**, not the trail, and warrants its own
   look before any conclusion. No live change is implied here.
