## 3. The BTMM Three-Phase Daily Cycle (INDUCE → TRAP → SHIFT)

This is the underlying logic of every trading day — crypto, forex, or indices:

```
Phase 1: INDUCE
  - Market makers accumulate positions inside a tight Asian range
  - Retail traders see a range and place stops just outside it
  - No tradeable signal yet

Phase 2: TRAP (Stop Hunt / Manipulation)
  - MM breaks one side of the range (false move) to trigger retail stops
  - This also generates the liquidity MM needs to fill their real position
  - The move is OPPOSITE to the real intended direction
  - Brinks Box = the box formed just before this false move executes
  - The 2nd leg of the M or W pattern forms here

Phase 3: SHIFT (Distribution / Real Move)
  - MM reverses and runs price in the real direction
  - This is the only phase worth trading
  - Entry: after the trap confirms (structure shift on lower TF)
  - Hold: 1–3 hours in the session window
```

### Daily Reset and High/Low of Day (HOD/LOD)
- **Daily High/Low resets at 17:00 EST (5 PM NY time)** — this is the start of the new trading day
- Crypto: The day begins at 17:00 EST Sunday through Friday
- HOD and LOD are set fresh each day from 17:00 EST
- The Asian session (17:00–01:00 EST) **sets the HOD and LOD for the next day's stop hunts**

## 4. The BTMM Weekly Cycle — The 3-Day Pattern

The 3-day cycle is visible on 15m, 60m, and 240m charts:

### Standard Uptrend Weekly Cycle
```
Monday    → FALSE MOVE (inducement) — often fades by Tuesday
Tuesday   → Level 1 rise (first leg up; 5/13 crossover)
Wednesday → Level 2 rise (continuation; 13/50 crossover)
Thursday  → Level 3 rise (final leg; can convert scalp to swing)
Friday    → Profit taking — erratic; MM cleans up positions
```

### Standard Downtrend Weekly Cycle (mirror image)
```
M → False move up (trap) → W → V1 → V2 → M (down cycle)
W → V1 → V2 → M (3 levels of drop)
```

### Weekly Trading Rules
- **Monday:** Treat as induction only — the "false start" for the week. Do not commit to trend trades.
- **Tue–Thu:** Core trading days. By Wednesday or Thursday, if the midweek reversal is identified, a scalp can be held as a swing.
- **Friday:** Do not hold trades over the weekend. MM books profits = erratic large moves.
- **PF (Peak Formation):** Once the weekly PF is established (usually Monday's false move extreme), all trades are taken **against the PF** in the real direction.
- **AWR Rule:** If the weekly range has already moved 80%+ of the Average Weekly Range, avoid counter-trend ADR trades — risk/reward deteriorates.

## 8. ICT Judas Swing — The False Move Model

This is the ICT name for what BTMM calls the "stop hunt" and TR calls the "Brinks Box false break." Same concept, different vocabulary.

### Definition
A **false, sharp move at session open** designed to trap retail traders before the real institutional move of the day begins.

### Exact Setup Flow
1. Asian session closes — range is defined
2. At session open (London or NY), price makes a sharp move **against** the daily bias
3. This spike targets the Asian range high or low (takes stops, provides liquidity)
4. Price **fails at the extreme** — reversal candle forms (pin bar, engulfing, or doji)
5. Price **reverses** and runs in the daily bias direction — the real move begins
6. Entry: after the fake move exhausts + structure break on 5m or 15m

### 1/3 ADR Rule
> *"The 1/3 ADR price level is deemed to be a threshold used to determine the area at which a Judas Swing might exhaust."*

- If price has moved approximately 1/3 of the Average Daily Range from the session open in the false direction, that is the exhaustion zone
- Watch for reversal signals at the 1/3 ADR level during the Brinks window
- This level often coincides with a previous session high/low or pivot level

### Timing
- **London Judas Swing:** 2:00–5:00 AM EST (London Kill Zone)
- **NY Judas Swing:** 8:30–9:45 AM EST (NY Brinks Zone)
- Duration from false move to real move: 30–60 minutes

## 9. Railroad Track (RRT) Pattern — Brinks Candle Variant

The Railroad Track is BTMM's fastest M/W pattern variant and the signature Brinks candle formation.

### Definition
Two consecutive candles of approximately **equal body length but opposite color**, forming back-to-back:
```
Bullish RRT:       Bearish RRT:
  [Red]              [Green]
  [Green]            [Red]
```

### Rules
- Both candles must be roughly the **same length** (symmetric)
- The second candle must NOT close higher than the first (bearish RRT) or lower (bullish RRT)
- This is the **fastest M/W formation:** 15 minutes down + 15 minutes up = complete

### Confirmation
- The third candle after the RRT should confirm the reversal direction
- A pin bar / Pinocchio candle pointing in the reversal direction = strong confirmation
- Volume: if a vector or climax candle appears on the second RRT candle = highest confidence

### Entry Rules
- **Bullish RRT:** Buy stop 2–5 pips above the high of the pattern; stop 2–5 pips below the low
- **Bearish RRT:** Sell stop 2–5 pips below the low; stop 2–5 pips above the high
- Risk:Reward minimum: 1:1 (initial) to 1:2 (target)

## 10. Stop Hunt Mechanics — Detailed Rules

Understanding the exact stop hunt sequence is critical for entry timing:

### Normal Stop Hunt
- Range: **25–50 pips** (or equivalent $) beyond the session high/low
- Duration: 15–30 minutes before reversal
- Volume: Often accompanied by a vector or climax candle at the extreme

### Extended Stop Hunt
> *"Price is extended beyond the normal 25–50 pip stop hunt when traders refuse to commit their funds."*
- Occurs when retail doesn't enter the trap fast enough
- Price goes **further** than expected — then reverses more sharply
- The second leg of the M/W pattern is usually the extended stop hunt
- This is actually MORE tradeable — the reversal will be stronger

### Stop Loss Placement for M/W Entries
- **Second leg entry** (safer): stop 7–10 pips outside the High/Low of Day (HOD/LOD)
- **Never** place stop inside the manipulation range
- After initial TP1 hit, move stop to **breakeven**

### 5:4:3:2:1 Position Scaling Rules
- Scale OUT of the trade in ratio: 5 units → reduce to 4 → 3 → 2 → 1
- Take partial profit at each TP level
- Move stop to breakeven after first partial take
- Let the final unit run to the full target

## 15. Equal Highs / Equal Lows — Liquidity Pool Targets

Institutions deliberately target **equal highs and equal lows** because retail traders cluster stops there.

### How to Identify
- Two or more swing highs at approximately the same price level = **equal highs** (buy stops above)
- Two or more swing lows at approximately the same price level = **equal lows** (sell stops below)
- Double tops and double bottoms are the **most obvious** liquidity pools

### Trading Rule
- **Do NOT** trade the breakout of equal highs/lows as a continuation
- **DO** treat it as a **stop hunt target** — price will sweep equal highs/lows, then reverse
- After the sweep: look for MSS + FVG/OB retest = entry in opposite direction

### Application to Crypto
- Bitcoin forms equal highs/lows frequently on H1 and H4 charts
- During the NY Brinks window, these levels are common stop-sweep targets
- Combined with a Brinks Box formation = highest-confidence reversal setup

## 19. Complete Daily Routine (Step-by-Step)

### Pre-Session Preparation (Before 2 AM EST)
1. Check **weekly bias** on D1/H4 — is the weekly PF established?
2. Mark **yesterday's HOD and LOD** — these are primary stop hunt targets
3. Calculate **ADR** — how much range is available today?
4. Check **economic calendar** — any high-impact news? Avoid 30 min around it
5. Identify any **unfilled FVGs** from yesterday or previous sessions
6. Note any **equal highs or equal lows** visible on H1 or H4

### London Brinks Window (2:00–5:00 AM EST)
1. Watch for **Asian range** to be swept (equal highs or lows targeted)
2. Look for **Brinks candle** forming — hammer/inverted hammer
3. Check if this is an **AMD Manipulation phase** in progress
4. Wait for the fake move to **exhaust at 1/3 ADR level**
5. If MSS appears + FVG/OB forms → prepare entry
6. Check for **Macro Window** (02:33–03:00 AM or 04:03–04:30 AM)
7. Enter on FVG/OB retest; stop beyond swept extreme
8. Target: previous day HOD/LOD, nearest pivot, or London session high

### NY Brinks Window (8:30–10:10 AM EST) — Primary Session
1. Identify the **London direction** — where did price go during London?
2. Watch for **NY Brinks stop sweep** (8:30–9:45 AM) of London session high or low
3. At 9:30 AM equities open: note the opening candle — it shows MM intent
4. Look for **M or W pattern** completing (second leg finishing)
5. Check **09:50–10:10 AM Macro** window for highest-probability entry
6. Confirm with **vector candle** in trade direction + above/below 50 EMA
7. Enter; stop 7–10 pips outside HOD/LOD
8. Target: opposite end of NY range, R2/R3 or S2/S3

### Trade Management
1. After TP1: move stop to breakeven
2. Scale out using 5:4:3:2:1 ratio
3. Watch for **Rule of Three** exhaustion on vector candles
4. When ADR is 80%+ consumed: close all or tighten stops aggressively
5. At 12 PM EST (London close): reassess direction; NY may reverse

### End of Day
1. Do not hold positions into Friday PM or over weekends (crypto exception only)
2. Log trade: entry, stop, target, result, which confluence rules fired
3. Review: did the Brinks Box set up correctly? Did the AMD pattern play out?
