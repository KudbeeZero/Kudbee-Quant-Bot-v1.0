# SECTION 1: STEVE MAURO BEAT THE MARKET MAKER (BTMM) — COMPLETE FRAMEWORK

## 1.1 The 5-Stage Market Maker Advance

BTMM describes the full price cycle in five stages:

### Stage 1 — Accumulation (Asian Session Range / Consolidation)
- **Visual:** Tight sideways range, typically under 50 pips on H1. Price oscillates with no directional conviction.
- **What MMs are doing:** Quietly filling large positions against retail flow. Need volume to build their book.
- **Identification:** Asian session range forms; does NOT break out decisively. Three small pushes within the range induce retail to take positions in wrong direction.
- **Duration:** Asian session, approximately 00:30–07:00 GMT. Ideal Asian range = **50 pips or less**; often contracts to 35–28 pips.

### Stage 2 — Manipulation / Stop Hunt (London Open)
- **Visual:** Price breaks sharply out of the Asian range — typically 25–50 pips beyond range boundary — in **3 distinct pushes or candles**. This is the "M" first leg (bearish trap) or "W" first leg (bullish trap).
- **What MMs are doing:** Running stop losses accumulated during Stage 1. Filling book at better prices. Looks "real" to retail.
- **Identification:** Rapid directional move, spread often widens, occurs between **1:00 AM and 4:00 AM EST**. Three candle pushes. Does NOT sustain — price stalls and consolidates 30–90 minutes after initial spike.
- **Key rule:** "A stop hunt is a false move out of the Asian range AGAINST the real intended move."

### Stage 3 — Distribution / Markup or Markdown (London Trend Run)
- **Visual:** After the stop hunt consolidates and forms the second leg of the M or W, price reverses and runs in the TRUE direction. **6–8 hour slow, steady channel** — the "trend run."
- **What MMs are doing:** Releasing accumulated positions in direction of real move. Price runs; MMs profit on positions built in Stages 1–2.
- **Identification:** Occurs after M or W second leg forms. Lower timeframe (15M) shows three consolidation levels (Level I, II, III). EMAs fan out in direction of move.

### Stage 4 — Markdown / Reversal (New York Session)
- **Visual:** Trend decelerates, range widens, "choppy" price action. Level III characteristics appear — wide ranges, difficult to trade.
- **What MMs are doing:** Taking profit. Beginning to accumulate positions for NEXT cycle.
- **Session:** New York, 13:30–21:00 GMT. NY reversal setups are smaller (40–50 pip targets) vs. primary London trade.

### Stage 5 — Re-Accumulation
- **Visual:** New sideways consolidation zone occurring WITHIN a larger trend. Consolidation at a mid-trend level before the next leg.
- **Distinction from Stage 1:** Re-accumulation happens INSIDE a larger move (at Level II or between cycles), not at absolute low/high.

## 1.2 Steve Mauro's 3-Position Model

Market makers simultaneously hold **three book positions** at all times:
1. **Long positions** (buy book)
2. **Short positions** (sell book)
3. **Flat/Neutral** (no position — waiting phase)

**The core mechanics:**
- MMs manage a two-sided book. Goal is to get retail to fill their book on BOTH sides.
- **Stage 1 Accumulation:** MMs induce retail to go long (3 upward pushes). Retail buys; MMs are sellers. MMs accumulate SHORT positions.
- **Stop Hunt (Stage 2):** Spike up confirms illusion for retail. More retail longs pile in. MMs continue selling. At top of spike, MMs FLIP — close shorts, begin accumulating longs.
- **Trend Run (Stage 3):** MMs hold long positions built during Stages 1–2. Price falls (if bearish cycle) or rises (if bullish). Retail longs get stopped out — MMs profit on both sides.

**Chart appearance:** The 3-position flip is visible as the transition from the first leg of the M/W to the second leg. The "pause" between the two legs (30–90 minutes) is the MM repositioning period — typically small consolidation or doji candles at the M/W second shoulder.

**The "3 swipes" rationale:** "The reason they use the number three is because traders are stubborn — they get hit one time and think it won't really go that way." Three pushes are needed to trap sufficient volume.

## 1.3 The "Money Zones" (Strike Zones)

**Definition:** A Money Zone is any price level where multiple confluence factors align:
1. Asian session range boundary (high or low)
2. Previous day's High of Day (HOD) or Low of Day (LOD)
3. Pivot point (daily)
4. ADR (Average Daily Range) extension target
5. Round number / psychological level
6. EMA 50, 200, or 800 as dynamic support/resistance
7. Previous week's high or low

**What price does when it enters a Money Zone:**
- Typically "stalls" for 1–3 candles (consolidation micro-structure)
- Forms a pin bar, spike, or doji as the "reaction" signal
- Then reverses with strong momentum (institutional entry candle)
- Stop hunts are almost always performed AT or TO a Money Zone, not randomly

**"Trading Zone":** Specifically the 25–50 pip zone BEYOND the Asian range as the primary Money Zone for the stop hunt trade.

**Strike Zone Checklist** (high-probability reversal — need 5+ factors):
- Asian range < 50 pips
- Three swipes out of consolidation
- Convergence with a pivot point
- M or W second leg forming
- EMA confluence (price touching 50/200/800)
- Previous HOD/LOD alignment
- ADR extension target hit
- TDI shark-fin or volatility band signal

## 1.4 BTMM Candle Patterns (Complete)

### A. M and W Patterns (Primary Setup Patterns)
- **M Pattern (Bearish):** Double top with second peak lower than or equal to first. Found at HOD. First leg = stop hunt up; second leg confirms the trap. Time between legs: **30–90 minutes** on H1.
- **W Pattern (Bullish):** Double bottom with second trough higher than or equal to first. Found at LOD.
- **Extended M/W:** Second leg goes slightly beyond first (false breakout beyond the false breakout) before reversing.
- **33-22, 3-33 patterns:** Multi-session variations where M or W plays out across separate sessions.

### B. Railroad Tracks (RRT)
- **Definition:** Accelerated M or W pattern. Specifically: **"15 minutes up followed by 15 minutes down"** on H1 chart, appearing as two opposing candles of similar size.
- **Purpose:** Tricks retail into trading direction of first candle. Second candle "snatches away" the move.
- **Entry rule:** Trade the DIRECTION of the second candle (the reversal direction), not the first.

### C. Spike Candles
- **Definition:** Large-bodied candles during the stop hunt phase. Often formed during news events or at session opens.
- **Rule:** "Designed to get you excited, trade emotionally... price is pulled back before the candle is closed." Do NOT trade the spike — it is the manipulation.
- **Identification:** Disproportionately large range vs. surrounding candles; closes near midpoint or toward the open (leaving a long wick).

### D. Pin Bars (Spike + Wick)
- **Definition:** Long wick in one direction with small body. Wick = stop hunt; body = rejection.
- **Entry criteria at Money Zones:**
  1. Pin bar forms AT a Money Zone
  2. Wick pierces the zone; body closes BACK inside
  3. Followed by second leg of M/W
  4. TDI confirmation (shark fin signal)

### E. Doji Candles (Consolidation Signal)
- **BTMM interpretation:** Doji candles in the M/W "pause zone" indicate MM repositioning — NOT traditional indecision. They signal the MM is filling the other side of the book.
- **Entry context:** A series of 2–3 doji candles at the M/W second shoulder, forming tight consolidation, is a precursor to the trend run.

### F. Half Batman Pattern
- **Definition:** Variation of M/W where only ONE leg forms. No second push. Price comes back to previous consolidation level and reverses DIRECTLY.
- **Condition:** Only valid when "adequate volume exists from a previous reversal."
- **Visual:** Looks like half of a "batman" silhouette — one wing instead of two.

### G. The Institutional Candle (IC)
- **Location:** Forms AT or immediately after a Money Zone. NEVER in middle of a move.
- **Body size:** Significantly larger than surrounding candles — typically 1.5–2x average candle body size.
- **Body-to-wick ratio:** Small wicks relative to body. Wicks at most 20–30% of total candle range.
- **Closes with conviction:** Full-bodied candle closing near high (bullish IC) or near low (bearish IC).
- **Breaks key structure:** IC often closes beyond a previous swing high/low or EMA level.
- **Timeframe:** Appears on H1 or H4 at transition from Stage 2 to Stage 3. On M15, it's the first full-bodied candle after the M/W second shoulder consolidation.

## 1.5 The Market Maker Trap — Exact Mechanics

**Trap Setup Step-by-Step:**

1. **Inducement phase (Asian range):** MMs push price 3 times within range, causing retail to enter longs (in a bearish trap scenario).
2. **False breakout (Stop Hunt):** Price breaks ABOVE clear resistance (previous HOD, round number, Asian range high) by 25–50 pips. Retail breakout traders enter long; short sellers' stops triggered. Massive buy-side volume created.
3. **The trap is set:** MMs have sold into the breakout. Retail is now long, MMs are net short.
4. **Reversal signal:** Price stalls at Money Zone. Spike candle, pin bar, or RRT forms. Volume stops.
5. **Second leg / Trend run:** Price reverses, dropping below the breakout level, triggering cascade of stop losses.

**How to distinguish Trap vs. Genuine Breakout:**

| Factor | Trap (MM Manipulation) | Genuine Breakout |
|---|---|---|
| Asian range | Tight, < 50 pips | Wider range or trending |
| Breakout move | Exactly 25–50 pips beyond range | Extends far beyond typical ADR |
| Candle structure | Spike, 3 pushes, wick rejection | Clean body candles, no rejection wicks |
| Time of day | 01:00–04:00 AM EST (London open) | Can occur anytime |
| TDI | Shark fin forms after push | No shark fin; TDI continues trending |
| EMA behavior | Price breaches EMA 50 then returns | Price closes above EMA 50/200 cleanly |
| Volume | Three distinct pushes, each losing momentum | Volume expands through breakout |

## 1.6 BTMM EMA System (Complete)

### The 5 EMAs and Their Names

| EMA Period | Color Name | Role |
|---|---|---|
| **5 EMA** | **Mustard** (Yellow) | Fastest signal line; short-term momentum |
| **13 EMA** | **Ketchup** (Red) | Signal line; medium-term momentum |
| **50 EMA** | **Water** (Light Blue/Aqua) | Intraday balance/trend line |
| **200 EMA** | **Mayo/Mayonnaise** (White) | Long-term trend definition; "Home base" |
| **800 EMA** | **Blueberry** (Dark Blue) | Macro cycle line; ultimate trend context |

### What Each EMA Represents
- **Mustard (5) + Ketchup (13):** The "signal lines." Their crossover gives earliest entry trigger. Fast, responsive, whipsaw prone.
- **Water (50):** "The intraday balance line showing the current trend. Price always returns to the 50." Critical dynamic support/resistance. During trend run, price pulls back to touch 50 EMA before continuing.
- **Mayo (200):** "Home base defining the longer term trend. Price always returns to home base." The 200 EMA is the magnet that price is always drawn back to on the daily timeframe.
- **Blueberry (800):** The macro cycle line. When ALL EMAs are below the 800, macro bias is bearish; all above = bullish. The 800 EMA crossing the 200 is the most powerful signal in the system.

### The Three EMA Levels (Cycle Count)

| Level | EMA Crossover | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **Level 1** | 13 EMA crosses 50 EMA | Beginning of new move; MM-driven, fastest level |
| **Level 2** | 50 EMA crosses 200 EMA | Mid-cycle; retail emotions dominate |
| **Level 3** | 200 EMA crosses 800 EMA (or 50 crosses 800) | End of cycle; exhaustion, chop, imminent reversal |

### Fanning Rules
- **Fanning = the EMAs spread apart from each other in the direction of the trend.**
- **Level 1:** EMAs begin to tighten/flatten, then 5/13 crosses — "flattening and tightening." This is the accumulation signal.
- **Level 3:** EMAs are maximally spread ("fan out"). Then they collapse back together, signaling end of the trend.
- **Trading rule:** Enter when EMAs are tightening (Level 1 crossover), ride during fanning phase (Levels 1→3), exit when fan collapses (Level 3 complete).

### EMA Crossover Entry Rules
- **Bullish cycle entry:** Wait for Ketchup (13) to cross above Water (50) — Level 1 confirmation. Price should be above 200 (Mayo) for confluence.
- **Bearish cycle entry:** Ketchup crosses below Water — Level 1 bearish confirmation.
- **High-conviction entries:** Wait for multiple EMAs to stack (all five in order: 5 > 13 > 50 > 200 > 800 for bullish; reverse for bearish).

### TDI (Traders Dynamic Index) Setup
Used in conjunction with EMAs:
- **RSI period:** 13, applied to Close
- **RSI levels:** 32 (oversold), 50 (median), 68 (overbought)
- **Signal line (Green):** 2-period SMA of RSI
- **Trigger line (Red):** 7-period SMA of RSI
- **Volatility bands (Blue):** 34-period Bollinger Bands, deviation 1.619
- **Market Base Line (Yellow):** 34-period SMA of RSI
- **Shark Fin signal:** When green RSI line exits the upper/lower volatility band (blue) and returns back inside — forming a "shark fin" shape. Primary entry confirmation signal for reversals.
- **"Blood" signal:** Extreme RSI readings (below 32 or above 68) with green line crossing red line — high-probability reversal entry.

## 2.1 Swing High and Swing Low Identification

### Classical 3-Candle Rule (Minimum Standard)
- **ICT Swing High:** 3-candle formation where the middle candle's high is higher than both adjacent candles.
- **ICT Swing Low:** Inverse — middle candle's low is lower than both adjacent candles.
- **Minimum candle count:** 3. Larger formations (5-candle, 7-candle) create **stronger** swing points.
- **Key teaching:** "A swing is defined by price reaction and intent, not candle count." 3-candle rule is the floor, not the ceiling.

### Confirmed vs. Unconfirmed

| Status | Condition |
|---|---|
| **Unconfirmed** | Middle candle has printed but the confirming (3rd) candle has not yet closed |
| **Confirmed** | The 3rd candle has closed, with its high lower (for swing high) or low higher (for swing low) than the pivot candle |

**Never use unconfirmed swings as BOS/CHoCH reference levels.**

### Major vs. Minor: The STH/ITH/LTH Hierarchy

| Level | Name | Formation Rule |
|---|---|---|
| **STH/STL** | Short Term High/Low | Basic 3-candle swing — smallest degree |
| **ITH/ITL** | Intermediate Term High/Low | An STH/STL with a lower STH (or higher STL) on EACH side — "swing of swings" |
| **LTH/LTL** | Long Term High/Low | An ITH/ITL with a lower ITH (or higher ITL) on each side — usually at HTF PD array |

**On 15M chart:** STH = 3-candle local high. ITH = high flanked by lower STHs on both sides. LTH aligns with Daily/4H level.

## 2.2 BOS (Break of Structure) — Complete Rules

### Exact Definition
A **BOS** occurs when price closes beyond the most recent swing point **in the direction of the prevailing trend**, confirming trend continuation.
- **Bullish BOS:** Price body closes ABOVE the most recent swing high (confirms uptrend continues)
- **Bearish BOS:** Price body closes BELOW the most recent swing low (confirms downtrend continues)

### Body Close vs. Wick — The Rule
**A BOS requires a candle BODY close beyond the swing level. A wick alone does NOT constitute a BOS.**

| Price Action | Classification | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Wick pierces swing high/low, body closes back | **Liquidity Sweep** | Stop hunt — NOT a BOS |
| Candle body closes beyond swing high/low | **Valid BOS** | Structural break confirmed |

### BOS Hierarchy

| Tier | Description | Significance |
|---|---|---|
| **External/Major BOS** | Breaks an ITH or LTH/LTL | High significance — confirms macro trend |
| **Internal/Minor BOS** | Breaks an STH or STL within a trend leg | Lower significance — confirms leg continuation |
| **Micro BOS** | Sub-5M structure break | Noise — not actionable on its own |

## 2.3 CHoCH (Change of Character) — Complete Rules

### Exact Definition
A **CHoCH** occurs when price closes beyond the most recent swing point **against the prevailing trend**, signaling potential reversal.
- **Bullish CHoCH** (bearish trend showing reversal): Price body closes ABOVE the most recent Lower High
- **Bearish CHoCH** (bullish trend showing reversal): Price body closes BELOW the most recent Higher Low

### Critical Rule
The CHoCH must break the swing point that was **created by the last BOS**, not just any arbitrary swing inside the structure.

### CHoCH vs. BOS

| Attribute | BOS | CHoCH |
|---|---|---|
| **Direction** | With the trend | Against the trend |
| **Signal type** | Continuation | Potential reversal |
| **Confidence** | High — trend intact | Lower — warning, not confirmed reversal |
| **Action** | Trade with trend | Prepare for potential reversal entry |

### Confirmed CHoCH Sequence
> **CHoCH → Pullback → Holds New Structure Point → BOS in New Direction = Confirmed Reversal**

A single CHoCH is never sufficient to call a trend reversal.

### CHoCH on Different Timeframes

| Timeframe | CHoCH Type | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Daily / Weekly | Major CHoCH | Macro narrative bias shift — holds for days/weeks |
| 4H / 1H | Intermediate CHoCH | Session directional bias — most traded |
| 15M / 5M | Minor CHoCH | Entry trigger within session |
| 1M / 3M | Fractal CHoCH | Micro entry refinement only |

## 2.4 MSS (Market Structure Shift) — Complete Rules

### Exact Definition
A **MSS** is a BOS in the **opposite direction to the prevailing trend**, accompanied by **aggressive displacement** — a sharp, impulsive move that creates a Fair Value Gap (FVG) and leaves a clear imbalance.

### MSS vs. BOS vs. CHoCH

| Attribute | BOS | CHoCH | MSS |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Direction** | With trend | Against trend | Against trend |
| **Speed required** | Gradual OK | Gradual OK | **Must be displacement/impulsive** |
| **FVG created** | Often | Sometimes | **Required** |
| **Liquidity sweep before** | Not required | Not required | **Required (sweep precedes MSS)** |
| **Signal** | Continuation | Soft reversal warning | Strong reversal confirmation |

### Precise MSS Trigger Conditions (All 4 Required)
1. **Prior liquidity sweep** — BSL or SSL taken before the break
2. **Body close beyond swing extreme** — must close clearly through, not just wick
3. **Displacement candle** — large-bodied, fast candle that engulfs prior structure
4. **FVG created** — the displacement move leaves a price gap (imbalance)

## 2.5 CISD (Change in State of Delivery) — Complete Rules

### Exact Definition
CISD occurs when the **candle body closes past the open** of the current delivery leg, indicating the institutional "delivery" direction has changed.

### The Core Candle Mechanic
- **Bullish CISD:** A down-delivery leg is interrupted when a candle's **body closes above the open of the initiating delivery candle** (first candle of the bearish leg).
- **Bearish CISD:** An up-delivery leg is interrupted when a candle's **body closes below the open of the initiating delivery candle.**

**Key distinction:** CISD never looks at swing highs or lows — it only looks at **candle opens and bodies.**

### CISD vs. MSS Firing Order

| Signal | Based On | Fires | Use Case |
|---|---|---|---|
| **CISD** | Candle body vs. delivery leg open | **Earlier** | Scalping, early entries, higher noise |
| **MSS** | Candle body vs. swing high/low extreme | **Later** | Confirmed reversals, medium-term |

CISD fires before MSS. Every MSS will have been preceded by a CISD; not every CISD will result in an MSS.

### Practical CISD Entry Rules
1. Identify the delivery leg: Mark the open of the first delivery candle
2. Wait for CISD candle: A body close back above (bullish) or below (bearish) that open
3. **Do NOT enter on the CISD candle itself** — it is a warning
4. Look for retracement into the body of the CISD candle or the FVG created
5. Enter on the retracement at the FVG, OB, or OTE zone (0.62–0.79 of displacement)
6. Stop: Below the CISD candle low (bullish) or above the high (bearish)
7. Target: Next structural level — MSS confirmation swing or next PD array

## 2.6 Inducement (IDM) in Structure

### Definition
**Inducement (IDM)** is a minor, engineered liquidity pool created by market structure that retail traders treat as a valid trade level — but which exists for smart money to sweep before delivering the real move.

### Where IDM Forms
- In a bullish trend: IDM forms as a **small swing low** between two Higher Highs. Smart money raids the sell stops below the IDM low, then delivers the real BOS upward.
- In a bearish trend: IDM forms as a **small swing high** between two Lower Lows. Smart money sweeps the buy stops above the IDM high, then delivers the real BOS downward.

### IDM → Real BOS Sequence (Bullish Example)
```
HH1 → price retraces → forms IDM (small swing low) → sweeps IDM low
→ grabs sell-side liquidity → BOS above HH1 = REAL structural break
```

### How to Avoid IDM Fakeouts
1. Never enter on the first break of a swing point — wait to see if it sweeps and reverses
2. Mark IDM levels explicitly after each BOS
3. Wait for the sweep of IDM + subsequent structure confirmation before entering
4. Body close confirmation: If price sweeps IDM but closes back (wick), it's the setup

# SECTION 5: ADVANCED RISK MANAGEMENT — KELLY CRITERION, MAE/MFE, VOLATILITY REGIMES

## 5.1 Kelly Criterion for Crypto Trading

### The Full Formula
```
f* = (b × p − q) / b
```
Where:
- `f*` = optimal fraction of capital to risk per trade
- `b` = reward-to-risk ratio (average win ÷ average loss)
- `p` = win probability (as decimal, e.g. 0.65)
- `q` = loss probability (1 − p)

**Worked Example — 65% Win Rate, 2:1 RR:**
```
f* = (2 × 0.65 − 0.35) / 2 = 0.95 / 2 = 0.4875 → 48.75% Full Kelly
```
Full Kelly says risk 48.75% of capital per trade — mathematically optimal but operationally suicidal.

### Fractional Kelly Variants

| Variant | Multiplier | Result (65%/2:1) | Growth Rate vs Full Kelly | Drawdown Profile |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Full Kelly | 1.0× | 48.75% | 100% (theoretical max) | 50–85% drawdowns historically |
| Half Kelly | 0.5× | 24.38% | ~75% | ~50% less variance, ~38% worst drawdown |
| Quarter Kelly | 0.25× | 12.19% | ~50% | Manageable even in black swan events |
| Tenth Kelly | 0.1× | 4.88% | ~25% | Near-zero ruin probability |

**Half Kelly property (mathematically derived, not a rule of thumb):** MacLean, Ziemba & Blazenko (1992) proved Half Kelly retains ~75% of maximum compound growth while cutting variance of growth by 75%.

### Crypto-Specific Rule
Use **Quarter Kelly (0.25×)** as the default for crypto session trading because:
1. Crypto exhibits fat tails and regime changes that violate Kelly's independence assumption
2. BTC/ETH have standard deviation of returns that routinely exceeds 2× the mean return (triggering the "use quarter Kelly or lower" threshold)
3. Correlated events invalidate per-trade independence Kelly requires

**Practical cap:** Never exceed 2% per trade as an additional safety layer, regardless of what Kelly suggests.

## 5.2 ATR-Based Position Sizing

### Core Formula
```
Position Size = Account Risk ($) / (ATR × Multiplier × Contract Value)
```

**Step-by-step:**
1. Set risk per trade: e.g. 1% of $50,000 = $500
2. Read 14-period ATR on entry timeframe: e.g. ATR = $2,500 on BTC
3. Choose multiplier based on session/regime (see below)
4. Divide: $500 / ($2,500 × 2.0) = 0.10 BTC position

### ATR Multiplier System

| Market Condition | Multiplier | Use Case |
|---|---|---|
| Scalping / tight setups | 1.5× ATR | Asian session, low-volatility periods |
| Standard session trading | **2.0–2.5× ATR** | **The sweet spot for kill zones** |
| Swing / wide structure | 3.0×+ ATR | News events, expansion moves |

### Session ATR Characteristics for BTC/ETH

| Session | Relative ATR | Position Size Implication |
|---|---|---|
| Asian (00:00–08:00 UTC) | Low baseline | Larger position size (same dollar risk, narrower stops) |
| London Open Kill Zone (07:00–10:00 UTC) | 40–60% above Asian baseline | Reduce position size proportionally |
| NY Open Kill Zone (13:00–16:00 UTC) | Highest of the day, 40% above London average | Smallest position size of the day |
| London/NY Overlap (13:00–17:00 UTC) | Peak volatility | Use 3×+ ATR multiplier or stand aside |

**Regime change rule:** Only adjust your standard ATR multiplier after three consecutive ATR readings above or below the 30-day average.

## 5.3 Volatility Regime Detection

### Primary Indicator: ATR Ratio (current ATR / 20-day average ATR)

| Regime | ATR Ratio | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Low Volatility | < 0.8× | Increase size by 25%; widen targets |
| Normal / Medium | 0.8×–1.2× | Standard sizing |
| High Volatility | > 1.2× | Reduce size 25–50%; tighten or skip |
| Extreme / Avoid | > 2.0× | Stand aside, no new positions |

### Position Size Multipliers by Regime
```
Adjusted Size = Base Size × Regime Multiplier

Low Volatility:   Base × 1.25
Normal:           Base × 1.00
High Volatility:  Base × 0.75
Extreme:          Base × 0.00 (no trade)
```

**Key signal for regime change:** Three consecutive ATR readings crossing the 80% or 120% threshold — not a single candle.

## 5.4 Maximum Adverse Excursion (MAE)

### Definition
MAE = maximum price movement against your position from entry before it hits the stop or closes in profit.

### MAE Formula (in R-multiples)
```
MAE_R (Long)  = (Entry − MAE Low)  / (Entry − Stop Loss)
MAE_R (Short) = (MAE High − Entry) / (Stop Loss − Entry)
```

### Stop Optimization Using MAE
**The 80th Percentile Rule:** After accumulating 50+ trades, plot the MAE histogram for winning trades only. Set your stop at the 80th percentile of winner MAE — meaning 80% of your historical winners would survive this stop placement.

**Decision rule:**
```
If current stop < 80th-percentile winner MAE → Stop is too tight, getting stopped out of winners
If current stop > 95th-percentile winner MAE → Stop is too wide, giving up too much R
```

**Optimization impact:** Optimizing based on MAE/MFE data has been shown to improve expectancy by 20–30% without changing the underlying strategy.

## 5.5 Maximum Favorable Excursion (MFE)

### Definition
MFE = maximum price movement in your favor from entry before the position closed.

### MFE Formula (in R-multiples)
```
MFE_R (Long)  = (MFE High − Entry)  / (Entry − Stop Loss)
MFE_R (Short) = (Entry − MFE Low)   / (Stop Loss − Entry)
```

### Using MFE for Profit Targets
**If your average actual profit is significantly lower than your average MFE, you are leaving money on the table.**

Practical rule:
- Set T1 (first target) at 60–70% of median winner MFE
- Set T2 (second target) at or just below the 90th percentile MFE
- Activate a trailing stop at 80% of median MFE for the runner

**MFE capture rate improvement:** This approach can lift capture rate from 40–50% to 65–75%, producing 30–60% P&L improvement.

### MFE/MAE Ratio as a Setup Quality Metric

| Ratio | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| > 3.0 | Excellent setup quality — price moves cleanly in your favor |
| 2.0–3.0 | Good setups — manageable retracement before follow-through |
| 1.0–2.0 | Marginal setups — nearly as much adverse movement as favorable |
| < 1.0 | Poor setup quality — reconsider the entry model |

**Use MFE/MAE per setup type:** Calculate the ratio separately for London kill zone entries vs. NY kill zone entries to allocate more risk budget to the better-performing session.

## 5.6 Portfolio Heat and Correlated Risk (BTC + ETH)

### Portfolio Heat Formula
```
Portfolio Heat = Σ (Risk per Position as % of Equity, across all open positions)
```

### Maximum Portfolio Heat Thresholds

| Trader Level | Max Heat |
|---|---|
| Conservative | 3–4% |
| Intermediate | 5–6% |
| Experienced | 6–8% |
| Professional Maximum | 10% |

### BTC/ETH Correlation Problem
BTC-ETH correlation exceeds 0.85 during risk-off events. During crypto market crashes, correlation spikes toward 1.0 — **your "two positions" effectively become one large position.**

### Correlation-Adjusted Position Sizing
For BTC + ETH (n=2, ρ̄=0.85):
```
Adjusted Weight = Base Weight × (1 / √1.85) = Base Weight × 0.735
```

**Practical rule:** When trading both BTC and ETH simultaneously, reduce each position to 73.5% of your standard size, or treat the combined trade as a single position capped at your single-trade maximum.

## 5.7 Drawdown Recovery Mathematics

### Core Formula
```
Required Recovery Gain = Drawdown% / (1 − Drawdown%)
```

### Full Drawdown Table

|---|---|
| 5% | 5.26% |
| 10% | 11.11% |
| 15% | 17.65% |
| 20% | 25.00% |
| 25% | 33.33% |
| 30% | 42.86% |
| 40% | 66.67% |
| 50% | 100.00% |

**The asymmetry is non-linear:** A 30% drawdown is 5–7× harder to recover than a 10% drawdown, not 3×.

### Minimum Required RR to Break Even at Your Win Rate

## 5.8 Pyramiding / Scaling In Rules

### Core Safety Principle
**"The risk of any new addition must be covered entirely by unrealized profit from the existing position."** Move the original stop to break-even before adding. Never add to a trade where the original position is still at full risk.

### The 1/3 Decreasing-Size Add Model
```
Entry 1: 100% of standard position size
Entry 2: 50% of Entry 1 size
Entry 3: 25–33% of Entry 1 size
Maximum: 3 entries total
```

### Exact Rules for Safe Pyramiding
1. **Trigger:** Only add after price moves 1R or 1× ATR in your favor
2. **Stop adjustment before adding:** Move original stop to break-even (or lock in 0.5R minimum) BEFORE placing the add
3. **Combined stop:** Set the stop on the added position at or above the entry price of the first position (for longs)
4. **Size reduction:** Each addition is smaller than the previous (50% reduction per add: 100 → 50 → 25 units)
5. **Total cap:** Entire pyramided position must not exceed 2× your standard position size in notional value

### When NOT to Add
- Already made maximum additions (3 total)
- Average entry is now close to current market price
- Price is approaching major resistance (T1 or T2 level)
- Volatility is expanding (ATR in high regime)
- Daily loss limit is 50% consumed
- In a correlated second position (BTC + ETH)

## 5.9 Break-Even Stop Rules

### Exact Trigger: The 1R Minimum Rule
**Do not move the stop to break-even until price has traveled at least 1R in your favor.**

Most strategies have winning trades that retrace 0.5–0.8R before going the right way. Moving to B/E before 1R means being stopped out by normal noise on valid setups.

### Recommended Trigger Levels

| Method | Trigger | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1R Rule | Price reaches 1× stop distance in profit | Minimum standard |
| 1.5R Rule | Price reaches 1.5× stop distance in profit | More conservative |
| ATR Filter | PnL > 2× ATR | Accounts for session volatility; best for crypto |
| Structure-Based | Price closes above/below a significant structural level | Requires discretion |

### Risks of Moving to B/E Too Early
- Increases apparent win rate while **decreasing actual expectancy**
- Triggers stops on normal market noise
- Can turn an inherently profitable trend-following system into a net-losing one

**Fundamental problem:** Break-even is a psychological decision, not a price-behavior decision. The correct trigger is structural (is the trade validated by price action?), not psychological (do I want to feel safe?).
