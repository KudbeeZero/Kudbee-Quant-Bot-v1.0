## 6. VERDICT (plain language)

**Sample / OOS / fees.** 137,326 resolved trades — 137× the 1,000 target.
Parameters were frozen at the validated live defaults and never refit to any
window, so all three regimes (2018 chop, 2022 chop, 2024-06→now) are
out-of-sample. Fees are modeled from gross through the full measured taker
(0.09% round-trip, MEMORY §25). Even restricting to the validated **1h**
timeframe alone we have **8,124 OOS trades** — 8× the target.

**Does the strategy have a positive, fee-survived, OOS expectancy at 1,000+ trades?**

- **Run indiscriminately across all timeframes: NO.** Pooled net-of-fees
  expectancy is **−0.019R** (bootstrap P(net≤0)=1.000). But that pool is **71%
  5m trades**, and 5m is net-dead. The pooled number is the wrong lens — it
  measures a book the live bot does not trade.
- **At the validated config (1h): YES.** **+0.096R net-of-maker, +0.060R
  net-of-FULL-taker**, n=8,124, bootstrap p<0.001. It clears the bar the way the
  live bot actually trades it.

**Does the edge survive the CURRENT cycle regime?** **Yes, clearly** — the recent
span (we are ~786 days post-halving) is the **strongest** 1h regime: +0.102R
maker / +0.064R taker, n=6,723, p<0.001. The current regime is not a problem for
the 1h book.

**Does it survive the CHOPPY cycle-analog regime?** **Yes, but thinner — and at
low confidence.** On 1h: 2018 chop +0.121/+0.085R (n=450), 2022 chop
+0.043/+0.019R (n=951). Both stay net-positive and taker-survived, but the edge
**dampens** in chop (the 2022 analog is the weakest, ~+0.02R net-taker) and
neither chop window is individually significant (small n). So: the edge is
**regime-dampened, not regime-broken** — consistent with the long-standing read
that confluence-R is "thinner in chop." Treat chop survival as *positive but
low-confidence*, not proven.

**Regime-FRAGILE flags (things that only work in some regimes / cost worlds):**

- **5m is dead in every regime** — net-negative at maker AND taker everywhere
  (−0.036/−0.003/−0.053 maker; −0.16/−0.10/−0.20 taker). The live 5m pause (§37)
  is vindicated. Never run 5m at size.
- **15m is maker-only** — net-positive at maker in every regime (+0.043/+0.028/
  +0.038) but net-NEGATIVE at taker in every regime. Its edge is entirely
  contingent on maker fills you cannot guarantee on a stop-out. Cost-fragile;
  do not add at size.
- **High-confluence 1h bands (70%/80%+) go negative in BOTH chop analogs**
  (2018 70-band −0.198, 2022 70-band −0.168). The "more confluence = safer"
  intuition is itself regime-fragile.

**The 60%-band question — does the forward "leak" repeat, and does dropping it
help?** **No, and raising the gate HURTS — in every regime.** On 1h the **50%
band is the best-performing band** (+0.103R) and per-R expectancy *falls* as the
band rises (60: +0.038, 70: +0.002). Cumulatively, moving the floor 0.5→0.6→0.7
lowers net expectancy in **every** regime (ALL: +0.096→+0.040→+0.005), and at
0.6 the **2022 chop analog flips negative** (+0.043→−0.020 maker, +0.019→−0.045
taker). This **refutes the autopsy's `--min-pct 0.6` idea out-of-sample** and
confirms the standing baton conclusion that the 0.6 tweak was in-sample/regime
luck. The 60%-band "leak" does not repeat as a fixable thing; if anything the
60% band is *worse* than the 50% band.

## 7. Config recommendation (NO live change — affirm + advise)

The cycle-aware OOS evidence **affirms the current live configuration**. Concretely:

1. **KEEP the live config exactly:** 1h · `min_pct=0.50` · `trend_align=True` ·
   3R target · 1.5-ATR stop (=1R) · 0.25-ATR maker retrace. It is the **only**
   configuration that is net-positive and full-taker-survived across all three
   regimes at a 1,000+-trade OOS sample.
2. **Do NOT raise `min_pct` to 0.6.** It lowers net-of-fees OOS expectancy in
   every regime and flips the chop analog negative. (Closes the baton's pending
   `--min-pct 0.6` decision: the OOS answer is *no*.)
3. **Keep 5m paused** (dead) and **do not add 15m at size** (maker-only / dies on
   taker).
4. **Size conservatively in the current regime.** The 1h net-of-taker cushion in
   a 2022-style chop is thin (~+0.02–0.06R); the post-halving chop regime argues
   for small size, not new signals — the edge is real but not fat.
5. **Watch-item:** the chop-analog 1h samples are small (450 / 951 trades) and
   not individually significant. The "survives chop" claim is positive-but-
   low-confidence; keep accruing forward 1h data before leaning on it.
